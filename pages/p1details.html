Hide and Seek

Ideation

When originally given the prompt of an application that takes sound as an input, my immediate reaction was to create some sort of game that uses screaming as an input. Even though screaming isn’t inherently something that brings delight, it can be made into something fun and delightful for the users!

My original idea was for a game that takes in the user’s scream as an input and then plays an animation that dynamically changes based on the length and volume of their scream. As I continued to ideate, I realized that this could be turned into something competitive that includes multiple players.

This idea took my original idea, but added a competitive twist to it, making it a game between two friends to see who could scream better than the other! The idea seemed like something fun that me and my friends would try out. However, as I started planning the implementation, I quickly realized that it would be incredibly difficult to program a method to isolate the two screams and have them only affect the character they are supposed to be in control of, so a new method would need to be devised.

This was the natural continuation of my scream fight idea, instead having the two users record their screams individually, they would record them one after another and then the application would put them against each other. I continued with this idea, but quickly realized that the scream visualization would require good artwork with advanced dynamic animations, something that I don’t have the skill to complete, so a different scream visualization would be needed.

This was basically the same premise as the turn-based scream fight, however it would simply use bars as visualization for the users’ screams. Once reaching this point in the ideation, I realized that my idea had almost entirely come back to my original idea! With that original idea in mind, I realized that instead of making a competitive app between two players, I could make something that is competitive between any number of players.

This application is much simpler of an idea than the earlier ones, but it carries the same sort of idea of a competitive screaming app. However it simply has users scream, and then the app gives the scream a score, along with a fancy visualization of their scream. The competitive aspect comes with the fact that all users can compare their scores between each other and figure out a winner that way. At this point, I was pretty finalized with my idea, however the dynamic bar visualizer proved to be a little too much for me to handle, so I had to simplify the visualizer aspect.

This is essentially the final idea I ended up going with and implementing. The idea is that the app would take in a scream, give it a score, and then hold onto that score if it was a high score. That way, a user could set a high score, and then their friends could compete to try and beat it.

Results and Instructions

This is the final product! Once it starts, it will take in screams and score them. The circle in the center dynamically changes size and colour in relation to the volume of the input it takes in. The score of the current scream, the score of the last scream, and the all-time high score are all stored in the corner and can be reset at any time using the reset button below them. The website is designed with mobile devices in mind, however it can be used on desktop as well, as shown in the screenshot above.

Overall, I think this project does bring delight, as I tested it with family members and friends and they had a lot of fun trying it out and trying to beat each others’ high scores. I also did some slight research into topics such as scream therapy and found out about the benefits of sometimes screaming your heart out, which my app encourages.
